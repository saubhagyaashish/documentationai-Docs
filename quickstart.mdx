## Prerequisites

<Callout kind="info">
Before you begin, ensure you have:
- A modern web browser like Chrome, Firefox, or Safari
- An email address for account verification
- Project documents ready (PDFs, Markdown files, or images)
</Callout>

## Create Your Account

Follow these steps to sign up for Saubhagya Ashish.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Visit the Sign-Up Page" icon="globe">
    Navigate to `https://app.saubhagyaashish.com/signup` in your browser.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter Your Details" icon="user">
    Provide your email, create a strong password, and complete the CAPTCHA.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verify Email" icon="mail">
    Check your inbox for a verification link from Saubhagya Ashish and click it to activate your account.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Log In and Create Your First Documentation Space

After verification, log in at `https://app.saubhagyaashish.com/login`.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Access Dashboard" icon="dashboard">
    Enter your credentials to reach the main dashboard.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create New Space" icon="plus">
    Click the `+ New Space` button in the top-right corner.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure Space" icon="settings">
    Name your space (e.g., "My Project Docs"), add a description, and select visibility (private or public).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save and Enter" icon="check-circle">
    Save the space and click `Enter` to view the editor interface.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Upload Your Initial Project Documents

Add your project files to the documentation space.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Drag & Drop" icon="upload">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Open File Explorer">
        In your space, expand the `File Explorer` sidebar.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Drag Files">
        Drag PDFs, images, or Markdown files from your desktop into the upload area.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Manual Upload" icon="cloud-upload">
    Click `Upload File`, select documents from your computer, and confirm.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Callout kind="tip">
Pro tip: Organize uploads into folders like `docs/api` or `images/screenshots` for better navigation.
</Callout>
